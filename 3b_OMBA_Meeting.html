Practicing PAGES and CALM

A warm-up: take an ordinary work meeting and reinvent it, one move at a time.

Prompt 1 · Break the meeting into parts
📋 Paste into the AI
I want to reinvent the meeting and find surprising new ways to design and run it. Let's start by breaking the meeting into its parts. List 8 parts of a typical work meeting, concrete and specific (not broad categories like "communication"). Spread them across three kinds: some about the physical setup (for example, the long table everyone sits around), some about what it signals about power and importance (for example, the seat at the head that marks who's in charge), and some about the social moment in the room (for example, the silence after someone asks "any questions?"). Number them 1–8. One sentence each.
✋ Your move

Read the 8 parts. Pick the ONE you want to change — ideally one you can picture redesigning the meeting around. Note its number; everything below builds on this single choice.

Prompt 2 · Change it three ways (Combine · Appropriate · Limit)
📋 Paste into the AI
I want to focus on part #⟨number⟩ from the list you just gave me. First, in one line, name the hidden assumption behind this part — what we take for granted about it. Then reinvent the part three ways, each a concrete change to the meeting I could see in a photo of the room: 1. Combine — fuse this part with something it normally has nothing to do with. 2. Appropriate — borrow how this is handled in a completely different domain (a courtroom, a jazz band, an ER, a dinner party…). 3. Limit — remove or shrink it until something has to give. One vivid line each, and keep every idea about this part.
✋ Your move

Pick the reinvented part that grabs you — just its number (1, 2, or 3).

🔮 Predict — pause here

Before you run the next prompt, make a bet: if you change just this one part, which element of the meeting will move the most — its Goal (what the meeting is for), its Event (when, where, and how it happens), or the Self (who you become in the room)? Hold your guess.

Prompt 3 · See how the other elements change
📋 Paste into the AI
I'm going with reinvention ⟨number⟩. Keep this new version of the part at the center. Show me how it forces the rest of the meeting to change: what new Goal does the meeting now serve, what new Event does it become (when and where and how it runs), and who do you become (Self) in the room? Then pull all of that into ONE coherent reinvented meeting, described concretely enough to picture. Keep it built on the part I chose.
✋ Your move + check your guess

Did the biggest shift land where you predicted — the Goal, the Event, or the Self? Either way, notice how changing one part pulled the others with it. You now have one reinvented meeting to take forward.

Prompt 4 · Push past the first good idea
📋 Paste into the AI
Push this meeting further. Give me two bolder versions that change what the meeting is for — not just how it looks or feels. Each should feel almost too strange to work, must still be driven by the part I chose earlier, and must break a real assumption about what a meeting is or does. One vivid line each, each a scene I could photograph.
✋ Your move

Pick the bolder version if it wins — or keep the earlier meeting if it still beats them. The point is to look past the first comfortable answer before you commit.

Prompt 5 · Name it & make the mockup
📋 Paste into the AI
Develop this one: #⟨number⟩. Give it a branded name and a one-sentence pitch that makes the idea unmistakable. Then create a realistic mockup image of the reinvented meeting — the room and the scene. Make the part I chose the thing the design visibly changes, and don't worry about legible text; image tools garble small print, so let the scene carry the concept.
💭 Reflect & share

Follow the instructions on the classroom screen to add your image to the shared document. Then write one line: which part you changed, and which element ended up moving the most. A change to one PAGES element changes how you think about the others.
